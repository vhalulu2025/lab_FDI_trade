## What has changed in U.S. export destinations from 2015 to 2025?

Canada and Mexico remain the top two U.S. export destinations in both 
years, with both growing substantially by 2025. China remains in the 
top 10 but exports to China declined noticeably, likely reflecting 
the impact of tariffs and U.S.-China trade tensions. The most notable 
new entrant is Taiwan, which joined the top 10 by 2025, reflecting 
growing semiconductor trade and supply chain diversification away from 
China. Actually, the United States is one of the primary origins for 
semiconductor imports to Taiwan. The United Kingdom saw notable export 
growth, roughly doubling from 2015 to 2025. Netherlands and South Korea 
also grew in importance as export destinations. Hong Kong and Belgium, 
which appeared in the 2015 top 10, dropped off the list by 2025.
